# Security & Custody

Security starts with clarity.

LiquidX should explain how deposits, allocation, dashboard tracking, and withdrawals work without claiming that funds are completely safe. No crypto product can honestly remove all risk.

## Custody model

LiquidX allocations are handled according to the platform's operational model.

Users deposit USDT through the official Telegram app. After confirmation and pack selection, capital may enter internal liquidity routes, managed pools, or vault structures.

LiquidX should communicate clearly:

* Which assets are accepted.
* Which networks are supported.
* Where deposits are sent.
* How dashboard balances are updated.
* How withdrawals are requested.
* What checks may happen before funds move.
* What risks remain.

## Account protection

LiquidX account protection may include:

* Telegram account verification.
* Confirmation for sensitive actions.
* Withdrawal address confirmation.
* Manual review for unusual activity.
* Anti-phishing warnings.
* Suspicious activity monitoring.
* Processing windows.

Security controls can reduce risk, but they cannot eliminate it.

## User safety

Users should:

* Use only the official bot: **@LiquidXBot**.
* Verify deposit addresses inside the app.
* Confirm the network before sending USDT.
* Keep wallet and exchange accounts secure.
* Ignore private messages asking for funds.
* Never share seed phrases or private keys.
* Keep records of deposits and withdrawals.

## Withdrawal address safety

Before withdrawing, always verify:

* Network.
* Address.
* Amount.
* Destination wallet.
* Any memo or tag requirement.
* Final confirmation screen.

Crypto withdrawals are usually irreversible.

## Custody risk

Custody and operational arrangements may involve wallet risk, key management risk, service provider risk, internal process risk, counterparty risk, and network risk.

Fund controls are important, but they are not insurance and do not guarantee capital protection.
