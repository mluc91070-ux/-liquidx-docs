# Official Bot & Anti-Phishing

There is only one official LiquidX bot:

**[@LiquidX_official_bot](https://t.me/LiquidX_official_bot)**

Any other bot, group, private message, support account, or website claiming to collect funds for LiquidX should be treated as suspicious unless it is verified through official channels.

## LiquidX will never

LiquidX will never:

* Message you first asking for funds.
* Ask for your seed phrase.
* Ask for your private key.
* Ask you to send funds to a personal wallet.
* Promise guaranteed returns.
* Ask you to bypass the official bot.
* Ask you to install suspicious software.
* Ask you to approve unknown wallet permissions.
* Ask you to pay a private unlocking fee for withdrawals.

## Before depositing

Always check:

* Exact Telegram handle.
* Official website links.
* Pinned messages.
* Official community announcements.
* Deposit address shown inside the official app.
* Supported network.
* Risk notices.

If unsure, do not send funds.

## Common scam patterns

Be careful of:

* Lookalike bot names.
* Fake admins in private messages.
* Fake support groups.
* Cloned websites.
* Screenshots promising guaranteed returns.
* Urgent deposit deadlines from unofficial accounts.
* Fake withdrawal unlock fees.
* Wallet approval links.

## If you suspect an impersonator

If you see a suspicious account:

* Do not send funds.
* Do not share personal information.
* Do not click unknown links.
* Report the account to Telegram.
* Contact support through the official LiquidX bot.
