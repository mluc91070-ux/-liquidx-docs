# Liquidity Captains

LiquidX ambassadors and KOL partners are called **Liquidity Captains**.

Their role is to educate, onboard, and grow local communities responsibly.

## What Liquidity Captains do

Liquidity Captains help users understand:

* What LiquidX is.
* Why liquidity matters.
* How deposits work.
* What packs are.
* How pools and vaults work.
* Where potential performance may come from.
* How withdrawals work.
* How referral rewards work.
* What risks users must understand.

The best Captains make complex liquidity concepts simple without hiding risk.

## Growth responsibilities

Liquidity Captains may support:

* Community education.
* Launch campaigns.
* Local-language onboarding.
* Telegram community activation.
* Referral growth.
* Product walkthroughs.
* Risk education.
* Feedback from users and markets.

They should direct users to official LiquidX channels and avoid acting as unofficial support for sensitive account issues.

## How Captains may earn

Strong KOLs and ambassadors may earn through referral and performance-based campaigns.

Campaign tracking may consider real metrics such as:

* Bot opens.
* Users joined.
* Pack selection.
* Confirmed deposits.
* Referral deposit volume.
* Retention.
* Community quality.
* Compliance with messaging rules.

Rewards are not guaranteed and may depend on campaign terms, fraud checks, quality review, and user behavior.

## Messaging standards

Liquidity Captains should not use language such as:

* Guaranteed yield.
* Fixed monthly profit.
* Risk-free.
* No loss possible.
* Easy money.
* Double your money.
* Passive income guaranteed.

The correct message is:

* LiquidX is a Telegram-native liquidity access layer.
* Users can start with simple packs.
* Performance is variable.
* Capital is at risk.
* Dashboard projections are illustrative.
* Users should read the docs before depositing.

## What makes a strong Captain

A strong Liquidity Captain educates before converting.

They build trust by being accurate, calm, transparent, and consistent. They do not spam, fake metrics, buy bots, or pressure users into deposits.
