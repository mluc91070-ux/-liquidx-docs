# FAQ

## What is LiquidX?

LiquidX is a Telegram-native liquidity access app.

Eligible users can deposit USDT, choose a liquidity pack, track their dashboard, invite others through referral, and request withdrawals from inside Telegram.

## Is LiquidX a trading app?

LiquidX is not a manual trading app.

Users do not place trades, use leverage, manage order books, or select individual market positions inside the app. LiquidX handles allocation through internal liquidity routes, pools, or vaults.

## Am I trading myself?

No.

LiquidX is not a manual trading app. Users do not need to trade. Users allocate capital through packs, and LiquidX manages the liquidity allocation layer.

## What is liquidity?

Liquidity is the ability to buy or sell an asset without causing a major price move.

Markets need liquidity so trades can execute efficiently, slippage can be reduced, and larger transactions can happen with less disruption.

## Why does liquidity generate fees?

Liquidity is useful because markets need capital to execute trades, support volume, reduce friction, and create market depth.

When liquidity is used in market activity, fees may be generated. Those fees may come from OTC execution, liquidity access, routing, spread capture, pool activity, stablecoin flow, or vault strategy performance.

## What is OTC?

OTC means over-the-counter.

OTC transactions happen directly between parties instead of through a public exchange order book. Large-volume transactions often use OTC because they need size, privacy, settlement coordination, or reduced market impact.

## How can OTC flow generate fees?

OTC desks need liquidity. LiquidX helps organize liquidity. When liquidity is used, fees may be generated. Those fees are part of the reward logic.

LiquidX may allocate liquidity to selected OTC-related routes. If OTC activity generates fees, those fees can contribute to vault performance and user rewards.

This may generate fees. It may contribute to rewards. It is not guaranteed.

## What is a vault?

A vault is an allocation structure.

It may group deposits, route funds into selected strategies, track performance, collect fees, support reward calculations, manage withdrawal cycles, and help apply risk controls.

A vault is not a guarantee.

## What is a Community Vault?

A Community Vault is a shared allocation structure where user deposits are grouped together.

This allows many small users to participate in liquidity activity together. Community Vaults can help LiquidX create stronger liquidity capacity than individual small deposits alone.

Community Vaults are not guaranteed-return products. They are allocation structures.

## What is a pool?

A pool groups capital so it can support selected liquidity needs.

Pools may support OTC liquidity demand, crypto market liquidity, stablecoin flows, market-making activity, commodities-related liquidity needs, private execution routes, or internal liquidity balancing.

## What is a pack?

A pack is a simple allocation tier.

LiquidX packs are:

* $100 Growth Pack.
* $300 Operator Pack.
* $500 Prime Pack.
* $1,000 Elite Pack.

Packs are not guaranteed-return products.

## Why should users buy a pack?

Users buy a pack because it gives them access to a simple allocation tier inside LiquidX.

A pack allows them to participate in liquidity activity, track their dashboard, and access referral rewards without managing complex market strategies themselves.

## What happens after I deposit?

Your USDT is credited to your LiquidX dashboard.

After you choose a pack, your capital can be allocated into LiquidX vaults, pools, or liquidity routes. You can then track your allocation, projected targets, rewards, referral rewards, and withdrawal status from the dashboard.

## Where does performance come from?

Performance may come from fees, spreads, routing activity, market-making activity, OTC-related liquidity demand, stablecoin flows, vault activity, or other eligible liquidity operations.

Performance depends on liquidity demand, OTC flow, market activity, vault performance, execution quality, counterparty behavior, costs, and risk conditions.

## Where do my rewards come from?

Rewards may come from fees generated by liquidity activity, including OTC flow, market liquidity, pool activity, routing fees, spread capture, stablecoin flow, and vault strategy performance.

Rewards are not guaranteed and depend on market conditions, product rules, pack selection, vault activity, fees, costs, risk controls, and withdrawal status.

## Are returns guaranteed?

No.

LiquidX does not guarantee returns. Performance is variable. Capital is at risk.

Dashboard projections and monthly targets are illustrative. They are not promises.

## Is my reward guaranteed?

No.

Rewards are variable. Capital is at risk. LiquidX does not guarantee returns.

## Can users withdraw?

Users can request withdrawals inside the LiquidX app.

Withdrawals may depend on pack rules, lock periods, vault cycles, liquidity availability, processing windows, security checks, compliance review, and network conditions.

## How does the referral system work?

Every user can invite others.

Referral rewards are based on real deposit activity and current program rules. Fake users, bots, spam traffic, and giveaway hunters should not be treated as qualified growth.

Good referrers educate users before they deposit.

## What are Liquidity Captains?

Liquidity Captains are LiquidX KOLs and ambassadors.

They help educate users, onboard communities, explain liquidity concepts, and grow local markets responsibly. Strong Captains may earn through referral or performance-based campaigns depending on real metrics and campaign rules.

## Why Telegram?

Telegram makes the product easier to access.

Users can open the Mini App, deposit USDT, choose a pack, track their dashboard, invite others, and request withdrawals from one familiar environment.

## Is LiquidX safe?

No crypto allocation product is completely safe.

LiquidX may use security controls, account checks, operational processes, and risk rules, but users remain exposed to market risk, liquidity risk, OTC execution risk, vault strategy risk, smart contract risk, counterparty risk, stablecoin risk, withdrawal timing risk, operational risk, and network risk.
