# Where Performance Comes From

LiquidX is built around liquidity-based operations.

Performance may come from several desk activities.

## 1. OTC volume operations

OTC means over-the-counter.

OTC trades are private trades between parties, often used for larger transactions that may not be efficient to execute directly on public order books.

Liquidity desks can generate revenue by facilitating volume, managing spreads, routing flow, and providing execution access.

Risks include counterparty risk, settlement delay, pricing risk, and liquidity risk.

## 2. Crypto market liquidity and market making

Market making means providing buy and sell liquidity to markets.

A market maker may place two-sided liquidity and capture spread when order flow is active.

Risks include market volatility, inventory imbalance, exchange risk, liquidation risk, and execution risk.

## 3. Fee-based liquidity opportunities

Some operators need fast access to liquidity.

LiquidX may participate in liquidity routing, access, or execution opportunities where fees are paid for liquidity availability or routing.

Risks include operational risk, counterparty risk, failed execution, and delayed settlement.

## 4. Commodities-linked liquidity

LiquidX may access liquidity opportunities connected to commodity-linked markets or operators.

This can diversify revenue sources, but it also introduces different risks, including settlement, pricing, counterparty, and market risk.

## Important note

Returns are generated by real desk activity, not by promises.

Markets move both ways.

Results can be positive or negative.
