# Vaults

Vaults are allocation structures used after deposits.

A vault may group capital, route funds into selected strategies, track performance, collect fees, support reward calculations, manage withdrawal cycles, and help apply risk controls.

A vault is not a guarantee.

## Community Vaults

A Community Vault is a shared allocation structure where user deposits are grouped together.

Community Vaults help LiquidX create stronger liquidity capacity than individual small deposits alone.

For example, one user depositing $25 is small. Thousands of users depositing $25, $50, $100, $300, $500, or $1,000 can create a meaningful liquidity pool.

Community Vaults allow small users to participate together in liquidity activity that would normally require larger capital.

## What a vault does

Depending on product rules, a vault may:

* Group user deposits.
* Route funds into selected liquidity strategies.
* Track performance at the allocation level.
* Collect or attribute fees.
* Support dashboard calculations.
* Support referral reward calculations.
* Manage withdrawal windows or cycles.
* Apply limits, reviews, or risk controls.

## Why vaults matter

Liquidity activity often needs structure.

Capital may need to be organized by pack, timing, strategy, route, risk limit, or withdrawal cycle. A vault can help LiquidX keep this process more organized than sending every deposit into a separate manual route.

Vaults also make the product easier for users. The user sees a pack, dashboard status, projected targets, rewards, and withdrawal state. The vault structure handles the allocation logic behind the scenes.

## What Community Vaults may support

Community Vaults may help support:

* OTC flow.
* Stablecoin liquidity.
* Market depth.
* Internal liquidity balancing.
* Pack-based allocation.
* Referral-driven community growth.
* Withdrawal cycle planning.
* Market liquidity strategies.
* Commodities-related liquidity activity.

The exact structure may change based on product design and risk controls.

## Vault performance

Vault performance may depend on liquidity demand, market activity, counterparties, execution quality, fees, costs, and risk limits.

Fees may come from OTC execution, liquidity access, spread capture, market routing, pool activity, stablecoin flow, or other selected liquidity activity.

If a vault route performs well, users may see rewards or positive activity on the dashboard. If conditions weaken or losses occur, results may be lower or negative.

## Vault rewards

Rewards may be calculated from eligible fees or performance generated by vault activity.

The calculation may depend on:

* Pack selected.
* Amount allocated.
* Vault activity.
* Liquidity demand.
* Market conditions.
* Product rules.
* Fees and costs.
* Risk controls.
* Withdrawal status.

Rewards are variable. They are not guaranteed.

## Vault withdrawals

Vaults may also affect withdrawal timing.

Some capital may need to complete a cycle, exit a route, pass security checks, or wait for available liquidity before withdrawal processing can complete.

This is why dashboard withdrawal status matters. Users should be able to see whether a withdrawal is requested, pending, processing, completed, delayed, or subject to a vault cycle.

## Important distinction

A vault is an allocation structure.

It is not capital protection, insurance, fixed yield, or a promise that every period will be profitable.
